# PG Mockup BA Preflight Rule v1

Status: active rule
Owner: Darrin
Applies to: CC-created screen mockups, Vellum mockup packages, and UI/UX approval materials before Darrin review

## Rule

Before any CC screen mockup is presented to Darrin, the mockup package must receive a BA preflight check.

BA preflight is a show-readiness gate, not final approval. A passing BA preflight means the mockup is suitable to show Darrin. Darrin remains the final approval authority for UI/UX direction, layout, copy, visual quality, and implementation permission.

## Required Flow

1. CC creates the mockup package.
2. CC or CD runs the BA preflight appropriate to the mockup format.
3. If BA reports fail/warn items affecting readability, workflow order, screen fit, visible feedback, forbidden language, or approval clarity, those issues must be fixed before Darrin sees the mockup.
4. If BA passes, CD may present the mockup to Darrin with the BA result attached or summarized.
5. No UI/UX implementation may begin from the mockup until Darrin approves it.

## Format Boundary

BA is strongest on structured HTML/browser-viewable mockups and applet/report surfaces. PNG-only or Vellum-specific mockups may require a mockup-specific BA adapter or Vellum-compatible preflight path. If BA cannot fully evaluate the format, the result must say what was checked and what remains manual.

## Protocol Boundary

This rule is not an implementation-go token and not a commit-go token. CD remains responsible for formal CC authorization tokens.
