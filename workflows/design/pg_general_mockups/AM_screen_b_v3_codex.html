Design Decisions

  • Purpose: Screen B is split into bug reading and bug action. The left pane renders parsed bug content; the right rail owns workflow, verdict, and valid actions.
  • Layout: Default and wide use two columns with a capped action rail. Narrow stacks the action rail below the bug content through the media query.
  • Stepper: Screen B uses an active per-bug stepper because it answers what happens next without adding instructional prose.
  • State card: The giant state rectangle becomes a compact verdict card. Work status remains separate so it does not duplicate the verdict.
  • Move actions: They are hidden on untriaged and running states. They return as quiet route utilities only after triage clarifies the bug.
  • Proposed Bible addition: clarify that dev-tool bug detail screens may use a parsed-content pane plus action rail when both reading and action are first-class tasks.
